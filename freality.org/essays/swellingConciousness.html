On the Edge Of Reality, Swelling Conciousness

Take a mid-day nap. And perhaps the strong drug of waking dream may overtake you. Wakeup to the sounds of something - your cat, your loved one - and you may be surprised to hear them before they make their sound.

A neural eruption is triggered in your aural processing and stray signals leak out to your cortex. Busy in its momentary dance with a thousand regions only ever named by doctors of disection in their peculiar Latin morphology, your dream is interrupted to invent something... anything... to describe this ambiguous sensory impression, which questions the verity of causality itself.

Robbed of a complete experience, and confused by the heavy drug of afternoon slumber, the on-shift logicians in the left hemisphere of your brain tell you it's time to wakeup. "Wakeup baby". They swear they heard it. But they've lied before. I know I heard it, but had anything really yet been spoken?

And then there it was again. The only utterance that could be agreed upon by a third-party observer; certainly the only one heard by our cats:

"Wakeup baby," she said.

Only later, on reflection, does our fabulously ornate latice of protein and salt begin to yield its secrets. Famously evasive of complete definition, it is still our own brain, still our own mind and still scrutible to itself.

Or at least so thinks the brain behind this essay.
