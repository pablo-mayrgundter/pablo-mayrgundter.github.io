"Thank God George Bush is our President!"


"And I say it again tonight..."

America deserves what it gets. For years we have let the idiocy fester. Now, it is prime time. The Republicans are indulging in patriotic safety fantasies at the RNC that have lost any semblance of adult thought, and they're eager to show it off. From sea to shining sea, their speeches ring out to the sincere adulation of pundits and couch potatoes alike.

This mental feebleness is exemplified by Former New York Mayor Giuliani. As he raked the ashes for his own political profits tonight, he conjured an image of Bush The Savior so amazing and angelic that to question its reality would be to miss the point entirely.

"There are times when leadership is the most important.

On September 11, this city and our nation faced the worst attack in our history.

On that day, we had to confront reality. For me, standing below the north tower and looking up and seeing the flames of hell and then realizing that I was actually seeing a man -- a human being -- jumping from the 101st or 102nd floor drove home to me that we were facing something beyond anything we had ever faced before.

We had to concentrate all of our energy, faith and hope to get through those first hours and days. And I will always remember that moment as we escaped the building we were trapped in at 75 Barclay Street and realized that things outside might be even worse than they were inside the building.

We did the best we could to communicate a message of calm and hope, as we stood on the pavement seeing a massive cloud rushing through the cavernous streets of lower Manhattan.

Our people were so brave in their response.

At the time, we believed we would be attacked many more times that day and in the days that followed. Spontaneously, I grabbed the arm of then Police Commissioner Bernard Kerik and said to Bernie, 'Thank God George Bush is our President.'

And I say it again tonight, 'Thank God George Bush is our President.'"

This is as tasteless as it gets. But it's not as stupid as it got.

Giuliani boiled our current foreign policy choices down to analogies with Hitler and nuclear annihilation, and asserted that Saddam, himself, was the Weapon of Mass Destruction. And then suddenly, pigs flew through the convention center.

Following this, the nuttiness was confirmed as Giuliani handed over the stage to a dead man; a posthumous performance of "New York, New York" by Old Blue Eyes to cap the night. Frankie would no doubt be a fan of such a canned spectacle, but somehow, that only makes it more surreal.

Add to this scene throngs of joyed-to-tears conventioneers and a battalion of riot-ready police and you have a vivid picture of Madison Sqaure Gardens Monday night.

Perhaps it would all be OK if the world existed inside of a rented convention space, and rhetoric could be substituted for reality at whim. But the vision of American destiny the Republicans are concocting in the dark corporate TV arena is in dangerous synchrony with their warlike terrorist counterparts. Like the protestors outside, most of us look on in disbelief and anger at our neighbors gone cuckoo.

Republicans, you're on warning. If by some miracle of judicial corruption, national blood-lust or just plain hopelessness you enter another four years of control, this country will turn into the freakshow you've been pining for, purely to spite you. You had better believe your apocalyptic bedtime stories, because we're calling your bluff. You've lost the civil goodwill. We can only ascribe so much to ignorance. You wanted this. Well, you're gonna get it.
